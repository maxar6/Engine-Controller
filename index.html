Element A

Presentation and Justification of the Problem

Each year crop yields are significantly affected by disease and insects. But, much of this loss of yield does not come directly from the diseases and insects, much of it comes from the inefficient methods that we use to detect and remove such ailments. Even in the most modern farms, a large portion of crops each year (12-24%) are crushed, or otherwise destroyed in early to mid-season by pesticide and herbicide sprays. This destruction is due to non-targeted spraying that, because of blanket coverage, has the tractor and the implement drive over much of the field wasting many chemicals, and destroying otherwise viable crops. Due to this non-targeted spraying, often times, the parts of the fields that are infected, are not covered properly to the extent that the spray doesn’t solve the problem.

Element B

Prior Solutions

The purpose of Element B, was to analyze prior attempts to solve a similar problem. The reason that the problems were similar, rather than previous products, is because this type of idea has never been done before. Within the element, we attempted to find products which contained elements that we could use to help develop our own improved product. While these drones are successful in their own manner, they do not fulfill the purposes intended, or are not successful in completing the task that we are trying to throw at it.
