Element A

Presentation and Justification of the Problem



Each year crop yields are significantly affected by disease and insects. But, much of this loss of yield does not come directly from the diseases and insects, much of it comes from the inefficient methods that we use to detect and remove such ailments. Even in the most modern farms, a large portion of crops each year (12-24%) are crushed, or otherwise destroyed in early to mid-season by pesticide and herbicide sprays. This destruction is due to non-targeted spraying that, because of blanket coverage, has the tractor and the implement drive over much of the field wasting many chemicals, and destroying otherwise viable crops. Due to this non-targeted spraying, often times, the parts of the fields that are infected, are not covered properly to the extent that the spray doesn’t solve the problem.

Element H

Prototype Testing and Data Collection Plan



The premise of element H is to begin planning how testing will work once the drone is completed. At this time the drone is not completed but this plan is being developed for the purpose of successfully being able to complete tests upon finishing the drones construction. The drone will be run through a series of tests; testing various things such as speed, stability, camera resolution, the ability to discern the colors and patterns, possible weight requirements.

The first test that will be completed will be the main stability test for this test we will place the drone on both covered and smooth surfaces to test take offs and landings. The drone will also need to be tested for in flight turbulence.

A test will also be done to find the optimal settings for the speed and camera speed, to get the clearest resolution in the pictures, to make the coding as accurate as possible.

The next test will be a flight speed test, this will be the most simple test seeing as it is simply taking the drone up to a height of 22 feet and flying out at maximum velocity. We will also need to test the drone to see what speed is the optimal speed for taking the clearest pictures for analysis.

What would follow that would be the video resolution test this will be done through taking the drone up to viewing height at approximately 20 feet bring it over a field of objects and different colors and shapes and then seeing at which speeds the drones are stable enough to record accurate data.

Once we have tested to drone for these key features we will have to test the function of the coding. Meaning we will have to make sure our code can decipher between certain colors and patterns as well as relaying the most accurate GPS coordinates as possible.

A very obvious restraint would be Drone weight. This could become a problem, but we have purchased motors and power supply that are far more powerful than is necessary so we have determined that it is only necessary to do a single takeoff test to guarantee that it work acceptably
